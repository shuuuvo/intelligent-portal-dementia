Taking Care of a Loved One

Taking care of a loved one with dementia is a daunting task that requires patience. There are a couple of strategies that you can use to reduce the frustration associated with the care of dementia peoties.

Create a Routine

The accomplishment of different daily tasks varies across the day. Therefore, performing tasks, such as bathing, during times when patients are most alert could be helpful. Allowing for schedule flexibility for particularly difficult days is also crucial

Involvement

Remember that despite the limitations associated with dementia, dementia patients can still perform some tasks, such as setting the table, with minimal assistance. Therefore, allow the person you are caring for to complete a significant part of the task at hand with minimal assistance

Provide Clear and Simple Instructions

Proving one-step communication safeguards against confusion and frustration

Limit Napping

Frequent napping undermines dementia patients ability to differentiate between day and night. Thus, limit multiple and/or prolonged naps

Reduce Distractions

Dementia impairs a person's ability to concentrate. Limiting distrations,by maybe switching off the TV, during conversations and mealtimes, to an extent, mitigates this effect

Remember that Dementia Progression Varies Across Individuals

Disease progression and the symptoms that dementia patients experience vary. Thus, you should tailor the provided tips to fit the needs of the person you are taking care of
